College life is hectic. Between lectures, assignments, group projects, and exams, staying organized is essential. A **course outline calendar** can help you keep track of everything—but with so many tools available, which one is right for you? In this guide, we compare the best **school calendar** and **course outline calendar** tools for college students.

## What to Look for in a Course Outline Calendar

Before we dive into specific tools, here's what matters most:

- **Syllabus import** – Can you upload a PDF or Word syllabus and auto-extract dates?
- **Multi-course support** – Does it handle multiple classes in one view?
- **Calendar sync** – Can you export to Google Calendar, Apple Calendar, or Outlook?
- **Mobile access** – Is there an app or mobile-friendly interface?
- **Cost** – Is it free, or does it have a reasonable student price?

## Google Calendar

Google Calendar is the default choice for many students. It's free, syncs across devices, and integrates with Gmail and Google Classroom.

**Pros:**
- Free and widely used
- Excellent mobile apps
- Easy sharing and collaboration
- Integrates with other Google tools

**Cons:**
- No automatic syllabus parsing—you must enter events manually
- Can get cluttered with multiple courses

**Best for:** Students who don't mind manual entry and already use Google products.

## Apple Calendar

Apple Calendar (formerly iCal) is the native calendar app for Mac, iPhone, and iPad users.

**Pros:**
- Seamless integration with Apple devices
- Clean, simple interface
- Supports .ics import for bulk events

**Cons:**
- No built-in syllabus parsing
- Less convenient for Windows or Android users

**Best for:** Students in the Apple ecosystem who want a clean, native experience.

## Notion

Notion has become popular as an all-in-one workspace. You can create databases for assignments and link them to a calendar view.

**Pros:**
- Flexible and customizable
- Great for note-taking and project management
- Calendar view available

**Cons:**
- Steeper learning curve
- No automatic syllabus import—manual setup required

**Best for:** Students who want a combined workspace for notes, tasks, and calendar.

## My Study Life

My Study Life is a free app designed for students. It includes classes, assignments, and exams in one place.

**Pros:**
- Free
- Student-focused design
- Cross-platform (web, iOS, Android)

**Cons:**
- No AI syllabus parsing
- Manual entry for each course

**Best for:** Students who prefer a dedicated study app over a general calendar.
