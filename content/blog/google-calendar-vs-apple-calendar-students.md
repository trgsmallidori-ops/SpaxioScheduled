Choosing the right **student calendar** app can make a big difference in how organized you stay during the semester. Two of the most popular options are Google Calendar and Apple Calendar. Both are capable, but they cater to different ecosystems and workflows. In this guide, we compare Google Calendar vs Apple Calendar for students.

## Google Calendar: The Cross-Platform Champion

Google Calendar is the default choice for many students—and for good reason. It works on virtually any device: Android, iPhone, Windows, Mac, and the web.

### Pros for Students

- **Universal access** – Log in from any device and see your schedule
- **Integrates with Gmail** – Event invites and flight confirmations auto-add to your calendar
- **Google Classroom integration** – Many schools use Classroom; assignments can sync
- **Sharing and collaboration** – Easy to share calendars with study groups or roommates
- **Reminders and notifications** – Customizable alerts for each event
- **Free** – No cost, no ads cluttering your view

### Cons

- **Manual entry** – No built-in syllabus parsing; you must add events yourself
- **Can feel cluttered** – With multiple calendars (personal, school, work), it can get busy

### Best For

Students who use Android, Windows, or a mix of devices. Also ideal if your school uses Google Workspace.

## Apple Calendar: The Native Apple Experience

Apple Calendar (formerly iCal) is the built-in calendar app for Mac, iPhone, and iPad. It's sleek, simple, and deeply integrated with the Apple ecosystem.

### Pros for Students

- **Seamless Apple integration** – Works with Siri, Reminders, and Focus modes
- **Clean design** – Minimalist interface, easy to read
- **.ics support** – Import calendar files from syllabus tools like [SpaxioScheduled](/signup)
- **Privacy** – Data stays in your Apple account
- **Offline access** – View and edit without internet (syncs when back online)

### Cons

- **Apple-only** – Best experience on Apple devices; Windows/Android support is limited
- **No syllabus parsing** – Manual entry or import from external tools
- **Less collaboration** – Sharing is possible but not as smooth as Google

### Best For

Students who live in the Apple ecosystem (Mac + iPhone + iPad) and prefer a clean, native experience.

## Feature Comparison

| Feature | Google Calendar | Apple Calendar |
|--------|-----------------|----------------|
| Cross-platform | Yes | Limited (Apple-first) |
| Syllabus import | Manual | Manual (.ics import) |
| Reminders | Yes | Yes (via Reminders app) |
| Sharing | Excellent | Good |
| Offline | Limited | Yes |
| Integrations | Gmail, Classroom, Meet | Siri, Reminders, Focus |
